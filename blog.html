Understanding Corporate Mergers and Acquisitions in Bangladesh

Advocate Kamal Hossain January 25, 2026
Introduction

Mergers and Acquisitions (M&A) are pivotal strategies for corporate growth and restructuring. In Bangladesh, the legal framework governing M&A is multifaceted, involving the Companies Act, 1994, and regulations from the Bangladesh Securities and Exchange Commission (BSEC). Understanding these complexities is crucial for any business leader considering a strategic partnership.

Key Legal Points
  • Regulatory Approval: Major deals often require approval from the High Court Division of the Supreme Court.
  • Due Diligence: A comprehensive legal audit of assets, liabilities, and intellectual property is non-negotiable.
  • Employment Transfer: Specific laws protect employee rights during the transfer of undertakings.
Practical Insight

For a smooth transaction, start early with a term sheet that clearly defines the valuation method and post-merger governance structure. Misalignment on these foundational elements is the most common cause of deal failure in the local market.

Legal Advisory Note

If your company is approaching a merger specifically to consolidate market share, be wary of the Competition Act, 2012. You must ensure the move doesn't violate anti-trust regulations.

Property Rights: Essential Guide for Homeowners in Bangladesh

Advocate Mohammad Ali January 20, 2026
Introduction

Owning a home is a dream for many, but disputes over title, mutation, and inheritance often turn that dream into a nightmare. Understanding your fundamental property rights is the first line of defense against unlawful claims and bureaucratic hurdles.

Key Legal Points
  • Title Deed (Dalil): Ensure you have the original registered deed. A certified copy is good, but the original is paramount.
  • Mutation (Namjari): Updating the Record of Rights in your name is separate from registration and equally important.
  • Possession: Legally, "possession is nine-tenths of the law." Secure your physical boundary immediately after purchase.
Practical Insight

Before purchasing land, always check the CS, SA, RS, and City Jorip records to ensure the chain of ownership is unbroken. A single gap in these records can lead to years of litigation.

Legal Advisory Note

If you receive a legal notice challenging your ownership, do not ignore it. A timely response drafted by a lawyer can often resolve the issue before it reaches court.

Navigating Divorce Proceedings: A Compassionate Legal Guide

Advocate Nusrat Jahan January 15, 2026
Introduction

Divorce is emotionally taxing, but understanding the legal process can provide a sense of control and clarity. In Bangladesh, family laws vary based on religion, and knowing which rules apply to you is the first step.

Key Legal Points
  • Notice: For Muslim marriages, a mandatory notice must be sent to the Chairman/Mayor and the spouse.
  • Arbitration: The law requires an arbitration council to attempt reconciliation within 90 days.
  • Dower (Mahr): All unpaid dower must be settled upon divorce, regardless of who initiated it.
Practical Insight

Prioritize the welfare of children. Courts increasingly favor shared parenting plans over strict custody battles, provided it serves the child's best interest. Document everything regarding financial contributions and child care.

Legal Advisory Note

Do not sign any "open-ended" settlement papers under pressure. Always have a lawyer review the terms of separation, especially regarding custody and alimony.

Your Rights When Facing Criminal Charges in Bangladesh

Advocate Rashid Ahmed January 10, 2026
Introduction

Facing a criminal charge is daunting. The power of the state is vast, but it is not absolute. The Constitution of Bangladesh guarantees specific rights to every accused person, ensuring a fair trial and protection from abuse.

Key Legal Points
  • Right to Counsel: You have the absolute right to consult a lawyer of your choice immediately upon arrest.
  • 24-Hour Rule: Police must produce an arrested person before a magistrate within 24 hours (excluding travel time).
  • Presumption of Innocence: You are innocent until proven guilty beyond a reasonable doubt.
Practical Insight

If arrested, remains calm. Do not sign any statement or "confession" without your lawyer present. Exercise your right to silence regarding the specifics of the alleged crime until you have legal representation.

Legal Advisory Note

Legal representation at the *bail stage* is critical. A properly moved bail petition can prevent weeks or months of unnecessary pre-trial detention.

New Tax Regulations 2026: What Businesses Need to Know

Advocate Tanvir Hassan January 5, 2026
Introduction

The Finance Act 2026 has introduced significant changes aimed at digitizing tax compliance and widening the tax net. For businesses, this means new reporting requirements and potentially new liabilities if not managed correctly.

Key Legal Points
  • Automated VAT Audits: The NBR is implementing AI-driven audits. Discrepancies in VAT returns will be flagged instantly.
  • Digital Services Tax: New withholding tax obligations for payments made to foreign software and ad services.
  • Green Tax Incentives: Tax rebates are available for factories adopting eco-friendly waste management systems.
Practical Insight

Review your vendor contracts, especially with foreign digital service providers, to ensure tax deduction at source is handled correctly. Implementing compliant accounting software is no longer optional; it's a necessity.

Legal Advisory Note

Non-compliance with the new digital reporting standards carries heavy penalties. Conduct an internal tax health check before the fiscal year-end.

Disclaimer: This article provides a summary of recent changes. For specific tax planning, consult a tax advisor or legal expert.

Intellectual Property Protection for Startups

Advocate Sabrina Chowdhury December 28, 2025
Introduction

For a startup, your idea is often your most valuable asset. However, in the race to launch, founders often neglect Intellectual Property (IP) protection, leaving them vulnerable to copycats and theft. Securing your IP early is essential for valuation and investment.

Key Legal Points
  • Trademark vs. Company Name: Registering your company name with RJSC does *not* give you trademark rights. You must register the brand separately.
  • NDA: Never pitch detailed technical specifications to investors or partners without a Non-Disclosure Agreement.
  • Copyright: Software code and original content are automatically protected, but registration strengthens your legal standing in court.
Practical Insight

Conduct a trademark search before designing your logo or printing marketing materials. Rebranding later due to infringement claims is costly and damaging to your reputation.

Legal Advisory Note

Ensure all employment contracts explicitly state that any IP created by employees during work hours belongs to the company, not the individual.

Disclaimer: This article is for informational purposes only. IP strategies vary by industry; consult an IP lawyer for a tailored plan.
